# Espresso Extraction
What the hot water actually pulls out of the coffee — and how to tell whether you pulled too little or too much.

| -[[Espresso Extraction]]- | → [[Espresso]]<br>: extraction — reading under- vs over-extraction |
| --- | --- |
| Related | [[Espresso Grind\|Grind]],  [[Espresso Ratio\|Ratio]],  [[Espresso Troubleshooting\|Troubleshooting]], |

Extraction is the share of the coffee's soluble mass that ends up in the cup; for espresso the well-balanced window sits roughly around 18–22%. Under-extraction tastes sour and thin because the fast-dissolving acids come out first and the sweetness hasn't followed yet. Over-extraction tastes bitter and hollow because the late, harsh compounds have been dragged out too. The two main dials are grind (surface area) and time, read together against the [[Espresso Ratio\|brew ratio]] — a sour shot usually wants a finer grind or longer pull, a bitter one the reverse.
